Biography

Dr. Thanana is a robotics and control researcher specializing in robust and adaptive control, data-driven modeling, human–robot interaction, and soft robotics. Since completing his Ph.D. in Science and Technlogy (Mechanical Engineering) in 2015, he has pursued several international research appointments. He has served as a Postdoctoral Researcher (2015–2017) and later as a Visiting Scholar (2018–present) at leading institutions including National Chung Cheng University (Taiwan), Huazhong University of Science and Technology (China), Guangdong Technion–Israel Institute of Technology (China), and King Mongkut’s University of Technology Thonburi (Thailand).

His research vision focuses on advancing uncertainty-aware and learning-enabled control frameworks that integrate model-based methods, model-free reinforcement learning, and Bayesian inference to achieve robust, adaptive, and safe robot behavior. Recent contributions include novel compliant control strategies for hydrogen-powered pneumatic artificial muscles (IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, 2022) and his current work on Deep Stochastic State-Space Models (Deep SSSM) for human–robot interaction and occlusion-robust visual manipulation. These efforts aim to bridge advanced control theory with data-driven learning to support next-generation applications in soft actuation, autonomous manipulation, human–robot collaboration, and large-scale cyber-physical systems.
