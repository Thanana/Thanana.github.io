Data-driven control

Sparse Bayesian Learning-based Adaptive Impedance Control

(Physical Human–Robot Interaction)

“Adaptive compliance through learning — robots that move in harmony with humans.”

Challenge physical effort and delay in human–robot collaboration, Change impedance dynamically through intention prediction, and create Impact in safe, responsive human–robot interaction.

Motivation

In physical human–robot interaction (pHRI), the robot must not only follow commands but also understand and adapt to the human partner’s intentions. Excessive operating force or delayed responses can increase fatigue and reduce safety. To minimize the human partner’s effort in both force and time, it is essential for the robot to continuously adjust its impedance parameters — stiffness and damping — based on the partner’s motion intention.

Framework Overview

This research proposes a data-driven adaptive impedance control (AIC) framework that integrates two learning-based modules:

  • Sparse Bayesian Learning-based Human Intention Predictor (SBL-HIP) — predicts the human partner’s next intended motion using time-series data of position, velocity, and interaction force.
  • Variable Impedance Controller (VIC) — dynamically modulates the robot’s impedance parameters based on the predicted intention, enabling compliant and coordinated behavior.

The overall control scheme operates as follows: The HIP predicts the future human action using minimal yet informative data, leveraging sparsity to ensure fast online computation. The predicted intention is then passed to the VIC, which adjusts the robot’s impedance parameters in real time. This allows the robot to anticipate human motion and respond proactively rather than reactively.

Application: Compliant Control

  • Robot manipulator equipped with a force sensor and compliant end-effector.
  • Human operator interacts physically through contact forces \( f_h \).
  • Impedance parameters (\( K, B \)) adapt online based on predicted intention.
  • Enhances cooperation smoothness, safety, and energy efficiency in shared tasks.

Validation

The proposed SBL-AIC scheme was evaluated through both simulation and experiment:

  • 2-DOF planar robot simulation — demonstrates adaptive impedance response under variable motion patterns.
  • 6-DOF manipulator experiment — confirms compliant motion and reduced operator effort during physical collaboration.

The results show that the proposed data-driven scheme enables robots to modify their dynamic behavior in real time, achieving natural and safe cooperation with humans.

This research bridges probabilistic learning and adaptive control — teaching robots to feel, anticipate, and act in synchrony with human partners.
