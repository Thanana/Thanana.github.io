Bridging Metal Hydride Storage and Quantum Transport: Toward Multiscale Modeling and Control

Prepare for submission.

  • Paper

  • Highlight

  • Code: powered by Julia

Hydrogen metal hydride (MH) systems offer high volumetric density and reversible storage for sustainable energy applications. Classical MH models, however, rely on empirical Arrhenius parameters that neglect quantum phenomena such as tunneling, zero-point motion, and lattice interactions, leading to large prediction errors at low temperatures. This paper proposes a unified multiscale framework that embeds quantum-informed hydrogen–lattice transport mechanisms into macroscopic diffusion and control models. The approach employs weighted–robust regression for parameter estimation, quantifies uncertainty via bootstrap analysis, and integrates the resulting quantum-corrected dynamics into a predictive control formulation. Numerical experiments demonstrate that tunneling-enhanced diffusion improves low-temperature response and reduces steady-state error and control effort by 35–50% compared with classical models. The framework thus bridges microscopic quantum transport and macroscopic control, advancing physics-consistent and data-driven control design for next-generation hydrogen energy systems.
