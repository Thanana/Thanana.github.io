Tactile Intelligence Loop

“To touch is to understand — robots that learn as they feel.”

Modern robots struggle with contact-rich tasks because friction, slip, and occlusion create uncertainty at the interface. The Tactile Intelligence Loop envisions a unified tactile-feedback architecture where sensing, inference, and control form a continuous cycle of understanding through touch.

Figure — Closed tactile feedback loop bridging tactile sensing, latent state inference, and uncertainty-aware RL–MPC control.

Motivation

I am deeply motivated by the challenge of endowing robots with the ability to feel, adapt, and decide through tactile interaction. My long-term vision is to bridge tactile sensing with policy optimization and model-based control— enabling robots to interact with the physical world as gracefully as humans do.

Building upon my prior research in robust, adaptive, and Bayesian model predictive control integrated with reinforcement learning, I aim to transform compliance from a mechanical property into an intelligent principle of adaptation. I aspire to advance this concept into a closed tactile intelligence loop—linking tactile perception, stochastic state inference, and uncertainty-aware control—to enable dexterous, human-compatible manipulation.

This pursuit reflects my broader philosophy of compliant intelligence—the idea that robots should not resist uncertainty, but yield and learn from it. By merging the physics of touch with the mathematics of control and learning, we move toward safe, intelligent, and interactive robotic systems.

Core Ideas

  • Latent tactile state: deep stochastic state-space models capturing uncertainty and temporal correlation.
  • Policy optimization: reinforcement learning for long-term adaptation, MPC for constraint satisfaction.
  • Bidirectional touch: surface haptics to actively modulate fingertip friction as both sensing and communication.

Objective

Develop robots that yield to uncertainty while maintaining stability, safety, and dexterity—advancing tactile robotics for human-compatible manipulation.
